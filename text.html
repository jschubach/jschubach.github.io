Writing 10.14

The discovery that the long-span spaces and the passing period are the most practical places to infuse wonder into the routine of the school day leads to the experiential objective of the project: that passing between classes brings you through a different world of material wonder. The structural system, the reciprocal corridor and spanning elements, then becomes a framework unifying different worlds of material wonder.

Writing 10.14B

The big challenge is how to collect these many material worlds into one building and create structural functionality and some semblance of aestheic unity.

Writing 10.13

Questions of construction relate to how to use the wiriness of steel for architectural expression - similar to questions of iron construction in the 19th century. How can a frame express architecture surface and multitude? Does it need to form surface, or is there another, more appropriate expression?

Writing 10.12

The project uses the circulation space to infuse wonder into the routine of the school schedule. Coupling the corridor with the long span spaces and distributing the long span spaces between clusters of classrooms means that students pass through another world between classes, the wonder-ful long span spaces.

Writing 09.29

The goal of the school is to create within the routine the sense of a space without limits, an enlarged space of possibilities made from the bounds that exist. How will I produce these spaces and how will they benefit eachother? How does the scheme set up these experience and relationships between these spaces?

Writing 09.28

How can layers of permanence and safety be created within the frame? How can this layering create expansive spaces on a tight site and a sense of wonder within the routine? Defects producing affects and effects, material inflections of use and production, the mixing of normal reality and routine with the alchemical creation of a sense of wonder. How to strike the perfect balance between flexible and distinct? How to convey the non-normative within the frame and to simultaneously establish aesthetic unity and a sense of the collective?

Abstract 09.14

The perceptible pressure to conform is often conveyed in the landscape of the American public high school through the emphasis on state-mandated standardized testing, monoliths of concrete block construction, and the push towards universally understood metrics of success. Within the discipline of architecture, we seek a similar standard of perfection and identicality in the contractorâ€™s execution of work. But good detailing depends on tolerances: no two students are the same, no two columns, and no two pieces of cladding. Systems that set up the conditions for success work with realities and tendencies. How can we use expansion and contraction - of the school district, the spatial field, and the detail - to inexpensively generate wonder and an expansive sense of possibility for high-school students today?

Cultural Context 05.25

In the words of the identically named Reddit thread, we live in a boring dystopia. Nowhere is this reality more evident than in public middle and high schools. How can this condition of monotony that characterizes the school experience be transfigured into one of possibility? How can we work with, not against, tight budgets and repetitive programs to generate a sense of wonder for these students today? Classroom posters say "Reach for the Stars", but how can the school show this spatially in a realistic and useful way?
